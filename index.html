PALITAW




Palitaw is flattened oval cakes made from malagkit rice paste, also used to make bilo-bilo for ginataan.
The kakanin gets heated in water and scraped out when it reaches the surface.
It then gets dusted with sugar and covered with shredded coconut.





PICHI-PICHI




Filipinos make pichi-pichi using cassava (kamoteng kahoy) or cassava flour, water, sugar, lye, and pandan flavoring.
It turns solid, transparent, and golden or light yellow in color when steamed.






KALAMAY




Filipinos prepare kalamay using glutinous rice flour, coconut milk, and sugar.
It has many variations, consistency, texture, and packaging depending on where you stay.
